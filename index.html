​​​​​The group works on a range of research topics, from understanding cognitive processes to perception-based engineering. The human understanding is derived and finds applications in domains such as serious games, empathy, neuroeconomics, (non)clinical assistive systems, urban planning interfaces, human intelligence, visual system & color and design of sensors.Spanning fundamental research to applied, the endeavor is to develop perceptive cognitive systems for positive social impact.

The serious games research focuses designing and developing software/hardware for visuo-motor rehabilitation post-stroke, spinal-cord injuries or any motor related degeneration. Systems validated by clinical tests are technology transferred to companies and have reached market. Models of spine-kinematics for Indian population as a function of body-mass and physical activity-level is being measured using motion-capture technique. The models will be used to build feedback mechanism for virtual agent assisted physical therapy in-addition to clinical use. The group also conducts research using games to understand human behavior in urban-life choices, driver behavior/traffic flow and financial risk-taking.

Seeking funds : The goal is to design and develop extremely low-cost neurorehab assistive systems to deploy in smaller towns and villages with remote data/diagnosis.

Neuroeconomoics research looks at risk-taking propensity and decision-making skills in financial transactions. Inhouse games applications are designed and developed in addition to applying traditional games like roulette wheel and centipede. Decision-making in personal/financial situations in urban-life choices is being studied using simulation games emulating real-life Health effects due to pollution, water levels and quality-of-life index. In general the role of trust, risk intelligence, situational factors, gender, education domain and emotion are of interest.

Objectification, empathy and violence on the female population is being studied at the neural (brain scanning) and behavioural (eye tracking, physiological) level. The aim is to understand factors such as socio-cultrual stereotyping and individual propensity for violence for example. Functional magnetic resonance imaging, EEG, skin conductance and eye tracking techniques are used to collect brain and behaviour responses using unique stimuli.

Plasticity during stroke recovery is analyzed using EEG technique and controlled motor actions. Here, we are also testing the effectiveness of using serious games for accelerating recover.

Low cost compact system to measure a number of eye related conditions - for example: amblyopia, strabismus, Ametropia, pupillometry and color vision.

Fundamental research on the visual system covers color perception and human-eye sensitively to high glare with focus on vehicle driver motor responses to high-beams.

Analytic reasoning gauged from fluid intelligence metrics and correlation to cognitive thinking styles is being pursued with special focus on role of education stream – STEM, fine arts, social sciences.

Prior studies included measuring the concept of ‘ quiet-eye’ and strategy as applied in badminton and caroom. Eye tracking was used to study scan/gaze differences of professional players and amateurs.
